Primary Keys

According to the Microsoft SQL Server developer reference, both Primary and Foreign Keys facilitate data integrity. The primary key is a way to ensure uniqueness among records. Sometimes these are sequential values, such as an incrementing counter. They may also be a combination of two values, such as Vendor ID and Product ID (VID/PID) or Serial Number and Model Number. In Microsoft SQL Server, a Primary Key can be comprised of up to 16 columns. Combined-column primary keys are also referred to as Composite Keys.

The primary key can also be thought of as an index value. The primary key will not repeat, but other columns can have repetitive data.

Other Keys

Foreign and Primary Keys are by far the most common. But there are other types of Keys that have stricter definitions, such as Candidate, Super/Composite, and Alternate keys.

Candidate Keys are another unique field respective to a record but does not refer to any specific data. Alternate Keys are essentially a secondary key. Consider a table that contains both a unique Student ID, and the Student's Email Address, or a table of MAC and IP addresses. In both cases, a duplicate value would cause a data integrity issue.
